Prep time: 5-10 min

Cook Time: 15-20 min

Rating: 4/5

Soondubu-Jjigae, or Korean Soft Tofu Stew, is a super quick and easy comfort meal to make at home!

It consists typically of a lot of seafood and highlights soft tofu, but as a budget college student, I do not have seafood readily available. This stew is super flexible, and you can add basically anything, so it's great if you're trying to clean out your fridge or just don't really have any food at home!

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Start heating the pot on the stove over medium low heat and add all the chili stuff as well as garlic. Stir them well for about 1 min. Make sure not to burn anything.
  2. Add your protein/seafood and onions and stir quickly to coat them with the chili sauce. Add the fish sauce and soy sauce then stir.
  3. Add water and gochujang and mix. Boil it on medium-high heat until it starts to boil rapidly (2 to 3 mins).
  4. Add the tofu, mushrooms, rice cakes, and egg (basically everything else) and cook them for another 2 to 3 mins. Season with salt if needed. You can also add ramen noodles in here if you want to eat them. If not, serve with rice.
  5. Once everything finishes cooking, top/garnish with the green onion, black pepper and sesame oil. I also like to add cheese at this step.
  6. Enjoy!
