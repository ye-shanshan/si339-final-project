Prep time: 10-15 min

Cook Time: 10-20 min

Rating: 4/5

Zha Jiang Mian (Beijing Fried Sauce Noodles) is a popular Beijing Dish that can easily be made at home!

A classic, budget-meal Beijing dish, Zha Jiang Mian features ground pork and onion cooked in a rich savory brown sauce with a hint of sweetness, tossed with noodles and then served with crunchy cucumber. It takes no time to prepare, is easy to cook, and can be prepared ahead of time. It's a perfect one-bowl dish for hot days and is great for meal prep as well.

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Start by preparing the Sweet bean sauce. It's a thick paste that's easy to burn in a hot pan. So you will need to add water to it to thin it out before cooking.
  2. Next, chop up your vegetables (onion and cucumber) into thin strips. Mince your ginger and garlic.
  3. Bring water to a boil and put in your noodles. Read the instructions on the packet to see how long they take to cook.
  4. To make the Zha Jiang Mian sauce, start by adding some oil to a pan and gently cook your minced ginger.
  5. Then, add in your ground pork and mix until cooked.
  6. Add the Sweet Bean Paste and simmer to bring out the flavor.
  7. Add in your onions and cook until tender.
  8. Your sauce is ready! Mix and add to your noodles along with sliced cucumber. Top with minced garlic.
  9. Enjoy!
