Prep time: 10-15 min

Cook Time: 20-25 min

Rating: 5/5

Korean Fried Chicken is super easy to customize and make different variations!

Korean Fried Chicken is just your normal fried chicken, but coated in a layer of Korean sauce. For this recipe, I made a sweeter soy-honey sauce but it can be changed according to taste!

WHAT YOU'LL NEED

MAIN

COATING/SAUCE

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Cut your chicken (if needed) into the size pieces you want. Pat dry.
  2. Beat an egg and dip your chicken in it.
  3. Mix salt and pepper to with flour and coat your chicken. Shake off the excess.
  4. Heat the oil to 340 degrees F over medium heat. If you don't have anything to measure temperature, I like to use the chopstick trick: put the tip of your chopstick in and if the oil starts bubbling around it, then it's ready!
  5. Gently lower the chicken away from you into the oil and fry it until fully cooked for about 5-10 minutes (this varies based on the size of your chicken ieces). Be sure not to overcrowd your pot.
  6. To make a soy-honey sauce, heat up a small frying pan and add some garlic and oil. Once the garlic aroma has come out, add in some soy sauce, a little bit of white vineger, seseame oil, and chili oil, some brown sugar, and honey. Mix it up and keep adding things until it tastes right. Keep this sauce over heat until it thickens into a sticky consistency, but be sure to regularly mix as to not burn it.
  7. Once it's done, coat your fried chicken in it, and you're done!
  8. Enjoy!
