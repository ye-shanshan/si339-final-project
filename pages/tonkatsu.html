Prep time: 5-10 min

Cook Time: 15-20 min

Rating: 5/5

Tonkatsu, or pork cutlet, is a fun and delicious dish to make at home!

Tonkatsu is a classic Japanese dish made of juicy, tender pork coated in crispy panko breadcrumbs and fried until golden brown. It's served with a sweet and tangy tonkatsu sauce that you can either make at home or buy at the store. (I buy it because who has time to make it? Just kidding, it's super easy to make too if you want to!) You'll typically see Tonkatsu on rice with a side of shredded cabbage, as everything goes together so nicely. This is super simple to make and wow your friends with!

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Pound the pork loin or pork chop flat until it's thin.
  2. Using scissors or a knife, cut slits into your flattened pork against the grain. This prevents it from curling up as it cooks.
  3. Next, add salt and pepper to your flour and coat your pork with it. Shake off the excess. This traps the moisture in and keeps your katsu juicy!
  4. Add about a tablespoon of flour to the egg and mix them together. This creates a thicker egg wash that allows for more breadcrumbs to stick to. Dip the pork into the egg and let the excess drip off.
  5. Transfer the pork to the panko bread crumbs and press firmly to create a thick layer of crust that won't flake off when frying. Be sure to cover any bare spots with breading.
  6. Heat the oil to 340 degrees F over medium heat. Medium heat is essential to cook the pork thoroughly without burning the bread crumbs. If you don't have anything to measure temperature, I like to use the chopstick trick: put the tip of your chopstick in and if the oil starts bubbling around it, then it's ready!
  7. Gently lower the pork cutlet away from you into the oil and fry it for about 5-10 minutes.
  8. Take it out and let it rest until room temperature.
  9. Clean the oil of any excess panko crumbs that fell off and place the katsu back in. By double frying, this will keep it extra crispy! Take it out when it's ready (about 5 min).
  10. Shred your cabbage and add some dressing. You can also use the tonkatsu sauce!
  11. Serve your tonkatsu no a bed of rice with a side of cabbage. Drizzle tonkatsu sauce on it.
  12. Enjoy!
